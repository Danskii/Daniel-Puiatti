This is the third post in my building a brand from scratch journey focuses on building a target audience. This series is most effective when read sequentially. Here's the first post in the series.
I knew my target audience, now I had to start reaching them

Well, what I did was start by identify a concept that people would like: in this instance it was cool photos. Everyone likes cool photos, like these, pretty cool right?

So I would use cool photos as the basis of finding my audience. The idea, as I imagined, would be to start creating lots and lots of cool photos to post weekly. Naturally, social media was my distribution platform of choice, Specifically Instagram. Cause Instagram = photos!

The idea in my head would play out like this: I would start by casting a WIDE net by posting lots and lots of photos. Naturally, some people would like my content. Those who liked my content would become the foundation of my audience. I would slowly look at what people liked and try to mimic that over and over and over.

Essentially what we have here is a type of content marketing where I'm creating content to help expose my brand to people.

I guess, really, this is sort of what you'd consider a marketing funnel, this whole process as a sort of representation of the first step of the marketing funnel, the first step of acquision is audience. the hope is to convert the individual from audience to interested to customer to brand advocate. Anyways that's getting ahead. Let's focus on step one of audience building, content marketing.

Anyways so the idea I had was great. Make cool pictures. OK, but HOW DANIEL? HOW?

Well, I'll tell you how I do it. Pretty simple really, just takes a bit o time and committment.

1. find stock images
2. use photo editing to overlay your brand on the image
3. format your images so they look good across all channels
4. schedule your content
5. rest
6. review
7. optimize

Over and over and over and over and over. And over.

Once you repeat this process a number of times you will eventually start to see pattenrns. Types of content which people like. Even people who seem to really like your content. You'll get followers and likes. This is your fledgling audience.

PART 3 SHOULD BE BUILDING A TARGET AUDIENCE


Alright, so I had the brand, the product and the website all ready to go, fancy tracking codes installed with analytics and A/B testing technology and newsletter signup form. Lovely I know. But I needed customers.

Customers do not manifest from the ether
This is where a stark realization took hold. All the time and effort I invested in building the aformationed product and website were meaningless unless I had customers.

Now, marketing. Now, advertising
Thus I began building customers. I decided I would, once a week, produce enough visual content featuring my brand so that I could publish a post on social daily.

Every Monday I sit down for an hour, source images, photoshop my logo onto them, schedule and publish content. I have been doing this for nearly two months now. Traffic is increasing minimally.

I decided I would suppliment this organic content with an advertising campaign. SO I build a funnel. I built some ads, I targeted some audiences and I let the campaign run. I optimized, refined and optimzied more, tested new creative, new copy. I let the campaign run.

Every day, slowly, more impressions, more awareness. Every week I spend another few hours creating organic content.

So far, one tshirt. That's all I sold. No one said building a brand from scratch would be easy.
